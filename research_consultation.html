Research Consultation with Professor Jane Charles

Research Topic

    My redefined topic will focus on the ethics of adding artificial intelligence into robotic systems. The topic has not changed much from my original, but, through the consultation, I was given a list of strategies to ensure that a source is credible. One example, includes researching the authors to check their backgrounds and whether they are an expert in their field. This can be done through Wikipedia to gain quick facts about the person or subject. Professor Charles also spoke about how sources could be credible even if they do not have an author. Examples of these sources are some government sites and university pages.

Intended Audience

    My main audience will also be business owners who may want to incorporate AI into their company.

Resources Identified

    I asked Mrs. Charles if she had some other recommendations for finding credible information other than advanced google search and credo reference. Some recommendations that she gave me included the Pop Culture Universe, the Health and Wellness database, Biography database, especially for expert works, and the free web with websites such as Wikipedia to fact check my sources.

Artifact Plan

    For my artifact, I planned on making a flyer pitch. I spoke with Professor Charles about my plans and how I would be able to site my sources. To site, she recommended that I add a “source to look” at section or create a presentation talking about the research process. She also recommended sending to another page that has a bibliography so that the sources aren’t on the flyer.
