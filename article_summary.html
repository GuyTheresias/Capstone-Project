AI and Ethics: Can We Trust What We Do Not Understand?

   Artificial intelligence (AI) is advancing rapidly, and its integration into robotics raises pressing ethical questions. Is it right to deploy AI-driven robots in warfare while human soldiers risk their lives? How will AI impact the integrity of hospitals and patient care? As AI becomes more prevalent—especially with the rise of chatbots like ChatGPT—society must address the moral and practical implications of these technologies.

The Need for Transparency in AI Decision-Making

   One of the biggest concerns in AI ethics is transparency. Researchers argue that when AI influences decisions affecting people’s lives—such as hiring, education, or healthcare—there must be legal and ethical accountability. For instance, AI hiring tools often learn from past human decisions, inheriting biases from hiring managers. This can lead to unfair outcomes where candidates are rejected not because of their skills but because the AI lacks diverse data.

   To address this, experts suggest that companies should hire professionals from diverse backgrounds to minimize bias in AI systems. Additionally, industry standards should mandate proper documentation and ethical frameworks for AI data. As AI researcher Rana el Kaliouby puts it, "We have a responsibility to drive [ethics-related] decisions, not only because it is the right thing to do for society but because it is the smart business decision."

Diversity in AI Development Matters

   One surprising revelation is that even some AI developers don’t fully understand how their own systems function. This highlights the urgent need for broader participation in AI development. Unfortunately, the tech industry still lacks diversity, with programming roles largely occupied by men. I see this disparity firsthand in my programming class, where female and minority representation remains low.

   One reason for this gap is the fear that AI will take jobs, combined with a lack of awareness about opportunities in tech. To counter this, we need more programs that introduce students to coding and AI collaboration, emphasizing how these tools can enhance innovation rather than replace human workers.

The Path Forward

   AI is here to stay, and its role in society will only grow. To ensure ethical development, we must push for greater transparency, industry-wide ethical standards, and diversity in AI programming. By involving more voices in AI development, we can create fairer, more responsible technologies that benefit everyone.
